The Adventurers Guild: Gamified Developer Marketplace

Companies post "quests," CS students complete them for XP/rewards. F-to-S ranking system. 500+ users. GirlScript Summer of Code 2025. Operating under Open Paws.

EdTech Gamification React

The Problem

There is a significant gap between academic CS education and industry needs. Students often lack real-world project experience, and traditional internships are competitive and limited in availability.

The Gamification Approach

We applied RPG mechanics to learning—using XP, levels, and achievements to motivate progress. Real-world projects become "quests" (client work, open-source contributions), and skill trees are mapped to industry demands. The platform also fosters peer collaboration and mentorship.

Technical Platform

Stack

Next.js with Prisma ORM and Neon Postgres. TypeScript throughout. Chose Neon over Supabase after the India block.

Ranking System

F → E → D → C → B → A → S progression. Higher ranks unlock more complex, higher-value quests. Merit-based access to client projects.

Revenue Model

Guild takes 15-30% service fee on completed commissions. Lean bootstrapped model built around Indian market compensation rates.

Open Paws Integration

The Adventurers Guild operates under Open Paws, creating a symbiotic relationship. Two-track system: 20 quality interns handle D+ rank work (backlog, features), bootcamp students handle F/E rank tasks (learning, guided projects). QA team: Open Paws founder + me + a mentor being hired + 1 senior dev. All under Open Paws India Pvt Ltd once registration completes.

How Claude Code Built This

PM-reviewed Phase 1 codebase audit through Claude Code. Each major feature — quest pipeline, XP calculation engine, client dashboard, admin panel — developed through structured Claude Code sessions. Financial model with Indian market rates built collaboratively. GirlScript Summer of Code brought 10 stars, 25 forks, and community validation.

Impact and Iteration

We've seen positive student outcomes in terms of projects completed and skills gained. Challenges included team coordination and scope management, which led us to pivot based on user feedback and learn valuable lessons in community building.

Future Vision

We plan to scale to multiple universities, establish industry partnerships for project sourcing, and explore monetization strategies like freemium models and B2B services.
